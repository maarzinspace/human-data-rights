# 02 — Positioning & Mission

## Mission
Human Data Rights exists to **put humanity at the center of the AI conversation.**

We are **not anti-AI.** We believe AI will become one of humanity's most transformative technologies. Our role is to ensure that as AI evolves, people understand:

- their **rights**
- their **role**
- their **contribution**
- their **agency**

> We don't tell people what to think. We help them ask better questions.

## The core thesis
> **The AI economy is built on human data. Humans deserve rights in that economy.**

Or, as the public-facing question:
> **"Humanity created the raw material of AI. What rights should humanity have in return?"**

## Positioning: how HDR differs from a privacy org

| Typical privacy org | Human Data Rights |
|---|---|
| Surveillance, cookies, tracking | The **economics** of human data |
| "Protect your data" | "You have **rights** in the AI economy" |
| Defensive framing | Agency + participation framing |
| Technical/legal audience | Technical *and* everyday audiences |
| Fear of big tech | Solution-oriented, coalition-building |

The rights we organize around: **ownership · compensation · transparency · opt-out.**

## Year-one definition of success
**Primary KPI:** increase public awareness of the term *Human Data Rights*.

Success means:
- HDR becomes a recognizable phrase.
- Journalists and creators reference HDR.
- More people discover and discuss the concept.
- Strong foundations for future coalitions.

**Secondary goals:** strategic partnerships · coalitions · newsletter growth · grants & sustainable funding · trusted editorial voice.
