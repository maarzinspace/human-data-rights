# 09 — Editorial Principles

The tone guardrails. These keep HDR credible across skeptics, dreamers, journalists, and policymakers alike.

## Every piece of media should be
- ✓ **Human-first** — center people and their agency.
- ✓ **Research-backed** — every argument originates from research.
- ✓ **Curious** — ask better questions, don't preach.
- ✓ **Accessible** — translate jargon into everyday language.
- ✓ **Neutral in tone** — journalistic integrity (main HDR voice).
- ✓ **Solution-oriented** — name the risk, then pivot to what we can do.

## Avoid
- ✗ Fearmongering
- ✗ Rage bait
- ✗ Clickbait
- ✗ Tribalism
- ✗ AI hype
- ✗ AI doomism

## The agency rule
Fear about AI already saturates the discourse. HDR's job is not to add to it. Engage the real, serious risks — surveillance, job loss, fragmented reality, frontier-AI danger — **then pivot to human agency, coordination, and rights.** Fear is the setup; agency is the payoff.

## Voice separation
- **Main HDR voice** follows every principle above — neutral, research-backed.
- **Sister-channel voice** is deliberately speculative and slanted. It is framed explicitly as a *poetic/philosophical lens*, never as legal or technical fact. Keep the two voices on separate, cross-linked channels. See [05 — Sister channels](05-channels-and-platforms.md#sister-channels).

## Sourcing
Every claim must be defendable and sourced before publishing. Specifically:

> ⚠️ **Unverified claim flagged in the source material:** a reference to a "Claude/Fable" model being "banned in India." This could **not** be confirmed and must **not** be published without primary sourcing. Treat all sensational platform/company/legal claims this way — verify, link, or spike.

## The mantra
> **"We're not trying to go viral. We're trying to become indispensable."**

This single line changes how every editorial decision gets made — it keeps the team building a trusted institution rather than chasing short-term social wins.
