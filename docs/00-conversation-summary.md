# 00 — Conversation Summary

A single-read summary of the founder ideation session that produced this strategy. For the *why* behind each call, see [01 — Thinking Pathways](01-thinking-pathways.md).

## What HDR is
A non-profit at the intersection of **AI, digital rights, and the economics of data ownership**, with lead coalition partner **IPTO**. Core belief: people should have rights — ownership, compensation, transparency, opt-out — over the human data that powers AI. A podcast and a full content system are launching.

## The seven decisions that came out of the session

1. **Position on economics, not privacy.** Every privacy org already owns surveillance/cookies/tracking. HDR's distinctive story is the *AI economy*: "The AI economy is built on human data. Humans deserve rights in that economy."

2. **Tone: neutral, journalistic, solution-oriented — no fearmongering.** Engage the real bleak-future arguments (Musk, Hinton, MIRI, Harari on surveillance) but always pivot to **human agency** and what we can do.

3. **Split the brand into two lanes.** The main HDR channel stays neutral and policy-grounded. A **separately branded sister channel** explores the speculative/spiritual/existential angle (AI as mirror/trickster/cosmic evolution; the "his AI God" thread). Hard boundary between them; cross-link, don't blend.

4. **The newsletter is the source of truth.** Everything starts from one weekly editorial answering *"What is this week's Human Question?"* It becomes a YouTube video essay, then fans out everywhere.

5. **Organize content by idea, not platform — 4 buckets:** NEWS (The Newsroom), EDUCATION (AI Explained Like You're 5), OPINION (the essay/newsletter), CONVERSATION (X / Reddit / podcast).

6. **Only two original pieces per week** — newsletter + video essay. Everything else is adaptation. Rule: **every long-form asset generates 10–20 short-form assets.**

7. **Partnerships are a first-class lane**, not an afterthought. Media is the engine; coalition-building (universities, creator groups, AI labs, NGOs, policy forums) is how HDR becomes a *movement*, not just a publication.

## The recurring formats
- **📰 The Newsroom** — AI news through the HDR lens (30–60s, timely).
- **🧠 AI Explained Like You're 5** — one concept, simply (evergreen).
- **🎥 Essay Clips** — idea-driven cuts from the weekly video essay.

## Year-one goal
Make **"Human Data Rights" a recognizable phrase** in public AI discourse — referenced by journalists and creators — while building partnerships, growing the newsletter, and establishing a trusted editorial voice.

## The mantra
> "We're not trying to go viral. We're trying to become indispensable."
