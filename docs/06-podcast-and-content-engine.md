# 06 — Podcast & Content Engine

## Podcast framing
The biggest mistake would be launching a podcast about *"data policy."* Nobody wants to listen to that.

**Frame it as a question, not a topic:** *"Who Owns the Future?"* / *"The Human Data Rights Podcast"* — every episode tackles **one provocative question.**

Example episodes:
- Did AI steal from everyone?
- Should humans get paid for training AI?
- Can AI create without creators?
- Is privacy already dead?
- Will AI create a new billionaire class?
- Why your memes might be worth money.

### Guest categories
- **Creators** — writers, musicians, artists, YouTubers.
- **Technologists** — AI founders, researchers, open-source builders.
- **Policy** — digital-rights lawyers, AI-governance experts, regulators.
- **Contrarians** — people who *disagree* with HDR. (Often the most interesting episodes.)

> Podcast is **Phase 2.** In Phase 1, the "long-form" slot is the YouTube video essay.

        1 LONG-FORM EPISODE / ESSAY  (~60 min)
                      │
   ┌────────────┬─────────────┬──────────────┬───────────┐

             (strong                      (pro
              opinions)                    audience)

**One recording session generates 20+ pieces of content.** This is the leverage rule in practice (see [04](04-editorial-ecosystem.md)).

## Content series ideas

- **"What Data Did You Give Away Today?"** — daily/weekly; break down an ordinary activity (ordering food, scrolling, using Maps, using ChatGPT) and explain what data it created.
- **"The AI Receipt"** — take a popular AI tool and ask: What data trained this? Who created it? Who got paid? Who didn't?
- **"Data Rights in 60 Seconds"** — fast educational reels.
- **"Humans Behind the Data"** — stories of creators, workers, journalists, musicians, and ordinary people whose work feeds AI systems.
