# 03 — Content Pillars & Buckets

Two lenses on the same content: **pillars** (the themes we own) and **buckets** (how a piece functions). Use pillars for *what to talk about*; use buckets for *what format/lane it belongs in*.

## The 5 Content Pillars (themes)

### Pillar 1 — Your Data Has Value
Most people don't realize every tweet, caption, Reddit post, review, photo, article, song, and conversation helps train AI worth billions.
- *"You trained ChatGPT. Nobody asked."*
- *"The most valuable thing you own isn't your house. It's your data."*
- *"Why AI companies need humanity more than humanity needs AI."*

### Pillar 2 — AI Economics *(the distinctive one)*
- Should people receive AI dividends?
- Who owns training data?
- If AI replaces jobs, who gets compensated?
- Can data become a new asset class?
- UBI funded by AI?

### Pillar 3 — Data Rights Explained Simply
Translate GDPR / consent / provenance / AI Act into everyday language.
- *"Imagine Spotify made money from your playlists forever."*
- *"Imagine a company photocopied your diary and sold it. That's basically how training data works."*

### Pillar 4 — The Future
Will AI pay humans? What is digital labor? Can humans own AI-generated wealth? Is data the new oil — or something else entirely? (Thought-leadership territory.)

### Pillar 5 — Culture *(where we win social)*
Connect data rights to music, artists, creators, influencers, memes, fandoms, gaming.
- *"Should musicians get paid when AI learns from them?"*
- *"Should fanfiction train AI?"*
- *"Can your Instagram aesthetic become someone else's AI product?"*

## The 4 Content Buckets (function)

Organize *output* by idea, not platform. Every platform ends up publishing a mix of these.

| Bucket | What it is | Primary home |
|---|---|---|
| **NEWS** | Current events through the HDR lens | 📰 The Newsroom (IG/Shorts) |
| **EDUCATION** | Concepts explained simply | 🧠 AI Explained Like You're 5 |
| **OPINION** | The defended take | Newsletter + Video Essay + Essay Clips |
| **CONVERSATION** | Dialogue & community | X · Reddit · Podcast (Phase 2) |

**Why buckets beat platforms:** saying "Instagram does X, LinkedIn does Y" fragments the message. Saying "this is a NEWS piece" or "this is an OPINION piece" keeps every channel pointed at the same core ideas, just through a different lens.

## Pillars × Buckets (how they combine)
A single Pillar-2 idea ("Should people get AI dividends?") can ship as:
- **EDUCATION:** "What is an AI dividend?" (AI Explained)
- **OPINION:** the weekly essay arguing for/against
- **NEWS:** reaction when a company/government floats a related policy
- **CONVERSATION:** an X thread + Reddit question + a podcast segment
