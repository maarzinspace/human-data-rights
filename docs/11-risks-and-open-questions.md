# 11 — Risks, Bottlenecks & Open Questions

A working document for the team to think through **together** before/around launch. The [strategy](../STRATEGY.md) covers *what we publish*; this covers *what makes it survive contact with reality*.

**How to use this:** go through each item in a working session. For each, agree an answer, an owner, and a "decide-by" date. Nothing here is a blocker by itself — but the items marked 🔴 are the ones most likely to break the plan if left unresolved.

Legend: 🔴 high impact · 🟡 decide before launch · ⚠️ risk to monitor.

---

## A. Bottlenecks — where the machine clogs

### A1. 🔴 The weekly video essay is the binding constraint
Everything downstream (10–20 assets) hangs off one 8–15 min essay produced *every week*. Scripting + recording + editing + motion graphics weekly is the single hardest commitment for a small team, and where most media orgs break.
- **Questions:** Is weekly long-form video genuinely sustainable in Phase 1? Should it be **bi-weekly video + weekly newsletter** instead? What's the realistic production time per essay?
- **Owner:** ____  **Decide by:** ____

### A2. 🔴 Maria is overloaded
Campaign Head + editorial direction + newsletter + video essays + podcast host + Reddit + **two sister channels (CCA + IAG)**. That's three brands on one person; one will starve the other.
- **Questions:** What does Maria *stop* doing? Do CCA/IAG pause until HDR is stable? Can editorial direction or hosting be delegated?
- **Owner:** ____  **Decide by:** ____

### A3. 🔴 The "create → distribute" gap
Abhishek *creates*, Sristi *distributes* — but who **cuts the 10 clips, designs the quote graphics, and writes the carousels**? That production-line role is unassigned; without it, "10–20 assets per essay" is aspirational.
- **Questions:** Who owns short-form editing/design? Hire, volunteer, intern, or tooling? Is there a template kit so it's fast?
- **Owner:** ____  **Decide by:** ____

### A4. 🟡 Reddit will resist promotion
"Daily discussion/question + AMAs" from an org account is what subreddits ban as self-promotion.
- **Proposed shift:** treat Reddit as a community-intelligence **input** (listening, research, validation), not a distribution channel. Distribute only where genuinely welcome.

## C. Risks — what can blow up

### C1. 🔴 "Neutral" vs. advocacy is a contradiction
HDR has a clear position (humans deserve rights/compensation) — that's advocacy, not neutrality. Claiming "neutral" invites "then why are you arguing for X?"
- **Proposed reframe:** **rigorous, fair, non-partisan, research-backed** — not "neutral." (Would update Principles in [STRATEGY.md](../STRATEGY.md) and [09](09-editorial-principles.md).)
- **Owner:** ____  **Decide by:** ____

### C2. ⚠️ Sister-channel reputational bleed
*Is AI God* and *Collective Conscious Agency* are run by the same person steering HDR's credibility with journalists/policymakers. "Separately branded" may not survive a reporter connecting them.
- **Questions:** How firewalled is this really — pseudonymous, disclosed, or a fully separate entity? What's the answer if a journalist asks?
- **Owner:** ____  **Decide by:** ____

### C3. ⚠️ Walk-the-talk exposure
HDR argues for data rights, consent, and transparency, so two things will be scrutinized:
- Does HDR use **AI tools** to make its own content (scripts, thumbnails, voiceover)? Need a public, consistent stance.
- What is HDR's **own privacy policy** for subscriber/audience data?
- **Owner:** ____  **Decide by:** ____

### C4. ⚠️ Legal exposure on company-specific claims
Series like "The AI Receipt" name companies re: "who didn't get paid." That can attract defamation/legal pressure (beyond the already-flagged Fable item).
- **Action:** define a sourcing + (light) legal-review bar for any company-specific claim.
- **Owner:** ____  **Decide by:** ____

### C5. ⚠️ Whitespace assumption is untested
The plan assumes the lane is open, but Jaron Lanier's "data dignity," Mozilla, EFF, Data & Society, DAIR, and Creative Commons all touch it.
- **Action:** one-page competitor/landscape scan — what's our distinct angle vs. each?
- **Owner:** ____  **Decide by:** ____

### C6. 🔴 Consistency is the whole strategy — and the hardest part
The retention loop ("next week's question") visibly *punishes* a missed week.
- **Mitigation:** build a **2–3 week content buffer before going live**, not week-to-week. Define a minimum-viable week (what ships even on a bad week).

## Quick-win recommendations (if we agree)
The cleanest fixes are structural and can be decided fast:
1. **Buffer 2–3 weeks of content before launch** (C6).
2. **Assign a clip/design owner** (A3).
3. **Downgrade Reddit to an input** (A4).
4. **Reframe "neutral" → "rigorous & fair"** (C1).
5. **Write down the funding + measurement model** (B1, B3).
6. **Decide weekly vs. bi-weekly video** (A1).

> These are starting points for discussion, not decisions. Fill in owners and dates as the team works through them.
