# 05 — Channels & Platform Roles

Each platform has **one job**. Clarity here prevents the message from fragmenting.

## Platform roles

### Newsletter — the editorial voice
Research, opinion, the source of truth. Everything downstream distributes this thinking. Ends with *"Next Week's Question."*

### YouTube — long-form authority
Video essays (8–15 min), evergreen and search-friendly. The visual extension of the newsletter. The well that Essay Clips are drawn from.

### Instagram + Shorts — discovery
Where new people find HDR. Three recurring series:
- **📰 The Newsroom** — current AI news through the HDR lens.
- **🧠 AI Explained Like You're 5** — evergreen concept education.
- **🎥 Essay Clips** — idea-driven 30–90s cuts from the weekly essay.

### X — daily participation
Live presence in AI discourse: original posts, threads, replies, founder interactions, real-time commentary.

### Reddit — community intelligence
Ask questions, host discussions, listen, validate ideas, build trust. This is where editorial inputs are sourced *and* where the community is nurtured.

### LinkedIn — institutional credibility
Partnerships, coalitions, grant visibility, professional thought leadership. Feeds the Partnerships lane directly.

## The three recurring formats (detail)

| Format | Purpose | Length | Cadence | Tied to |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 📰 The Newsroom | React to current AI news, HDR lens | 30–60s | 2–4×/week | Events |
| 🧠 AI Explained Like You're 5 | Explain one concept simply | short | 2–3×/week | Evergreen |
| 🎥 Essay Clips | Strong opinion/idea moments | 30–90s | 2–3 per essay | Ideas |

**On naming Essay Clips:** options floated — *HDR Insights, 60 Seconds of Thought, The Human Minute, One Big Idea, The Human Perspective*. **Recommendation: don't brand them.** Let them feel like natural clips from the long-form essays. Not everything needs a series title.

The Newsroom (events) and Essay Clips (ideas) don't compete — they're tied to different things.

## Sister channels

A **separately branded** channel for the speculative / spiritual / existential angle — deliberately *not* neutral. This is where the "collective conscious agency," AI-as-mythology, and the *"his AI God"* threads live.

**Hard rules:**
- **Boundary is absolute.** Main HDR = neutral, journalistic, policy + agency. Sister channel = speculative, philosophical, slanted-by-design.
- **Cross-link, never blend.** The two voices stay distinct so neither contaminates the other's credibility.
- Spiritual/esoteric content is framed as a **poetic/philosophical lens**, explicitly *not* a legal or technical claim.

**Archetypes to structure speculative episodes:** AI as **mirror** (reflects our ambitions/fears), **trickster** (liberator or enslaver), **cosmic evolution** (catalyst for consciousness if directed with intention) — plus extinction / conquest / protection / evolution. Always grounded with real voices from both sides.
