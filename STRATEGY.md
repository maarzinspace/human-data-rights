# Human Data Rights — Editorial & Media Strategy (Phase 1)

*Phase 1 | July–August 2026*

> **Objective:** Establish Human Data Rights as a recognizable term in public AI discourse through consistent, high-quality editorial media.

This is the full strategy in document form. For the decision-by-decision reasoning behind it, see [`docs/01-thinking-pathways.md`](docs/01-thinking-pathways.md); for the per-section detail, see the numbered docs in [`docs/`](docs/).

---

## 1. Mission

Human Data Rights (HDR) exists to **put humanity at the center of the AI conversation.**

We are **not anti-AI.** We believe AI will become one of humanity's most transformative technologies. Our role is to ensure that as AI evolves, people understand their **rights, role, contribution, and agency.**

> We don't tell people what to think. We help them ask better questions.

HDR is a non-profit working at the intersection of **AI, digital rights, and the economics of data ownership**, with lead coalition partner **IPTO**.

---

## 2. Positioning

Most organizations in this space are *privacy* organizations — surveillance, cookies, tracking, big tech. That category is crowded and tired. HDR tells a bigger story:

> **The AI economy is built on human data. Humans deserve rights in that economy.**

Or, put as the question that anchors everything we publish:

> **"Humanity created the raw material of AI. What rights should humanity have in return?"**

The rights we organize around are **ownership, compensation, transparency, and opt-out.** Privacy is a sub-topic, not the headline. The economic framing is under-discussed, highly shareable, and engageable by technical and non-technical audiences alike.

---

## 3. Editorial Philosophy

Everything begins with one question: **"What is this week's Human Question?"**

- Every opinion should be **defendable.**
- Every argument should originate from **research.**
- The **newsletter is the editorial source of truth.**
- Everything else **distributes** that thinking.

We engage the real, serious risks of AI — surveillance, job loss, fragmented reality, frontier-AI danger — but we never simply amplify fear. Fear about AI already saturates the discourse. Our job is to name the risk and then pivot to **human agency**: here is the reality, and here is what we can do. Fear is the setup; agency is the payoff.

## 4. The Editorial Ecosystem (the "Media OS")

The whole system runs on **two original pieces of work per week** — a newsletter and a video essay. Everything else is **adaptation, not creation.**

```

                   ▼
        WEEKLY EDITORIAL MEETING

       (research · opinion · the take;
        ends with "Next Week's Question")
                   │
                   ▼
          YOUTUBE VIDEO ESSAY         ← the visual editorial
                   │
   ┌──────────┬────────────┬───────────┬──────────────┐
   ▼          ▼            ▼           ▼              ▼
 Instagram   X + Reddit  LinkedIn   Partnerships   (Phase 2)
 Discovery   Conversation Institution Coalitions    HDR Podcast
   │
```

**The leverage rule:** *every long-form asset should generate at least 10–20 short-form assets.* The newsletter is the engine; each platform is a different lens on the same core idea. We're not making more work — we're extracting more value from one piece of thinking.

**Retention by design:** every newsletter ends by naming **next week's question** (e.g. *This week: Who owns human data? → Next week: Can AI create original ideas?*), turning a publication into a conversation people return to.

## 5. Content: Pillars and Buckets

We use two lenses. **Pillars** are the themes we own; **buckets** are how a piece functions.

**The five pillars:**
1. **Your Data Has Value** — every post, photo, song, and conversation helps train AI worth billions.
2. **AI Economics** *(our distinctive territory)* — AI dividends, who owns training data, compensation when AI replaces jobs, data as an asset class, UBI funded by AI.
3. **Data Rights Explained Simply** — translate GDPR, consent, provenance, and the AI Act into everyday language.
4. **The Future** — digital labor, can humans own AI-generated wealth, is data the new oil.
5. **Culture** *(where we win social)* — music, artists, creators, memes, fandoms, gaming.

**The four buckets** (organize output by *idea*, not platform):

| Bucket | What it is | Primary home |
|---|---|---|
| **News** | Current events through the HDR lens | 📰 The Newsroom |
| **Education** | Concepts explained simply | 🧠 AI Explained Like You're 5 |
| **Opinion** | The defended take | Newsletter · Video Essay · Essay Clips |
| **Conversation** | Dialogue & community | X · Reddit · Podcast (Phase 2) |

Organizing by bucket keeps every channel pointed at the same core ideas, rather than fragmenting the message platform by platform.

## 6. Channels & Platform Roles

Each platform has **one job:**

- **Newsletter** — the editorial voice; research, opinion, source of truth.
- **YouTube** — long-form video essays; evergreen, search-friendly; the visual extension of the newsletter.
- **Instagram + Shorts** — discovery, via three recurring series.
- **X** — daily participation in AI discourse (posts, threads, replies, live commentary).
- **Reddit** — community intelligence (ask, host, listen, validate, build trust); also a source of editorial inputs.
- **LinkedIn** — institutional credibility; partnerships, coalitions, grant visibility.

**The three recurring formats:**

| Format | Purpose | Length | Cadence | Tied to |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 📰 The Newsroom | React to current AI news, HDR lens | 30–60s | 2–4×/week | Events |
| 🧠 AI Explained Like You're 5 | Explain one concept simply | short | 2–3×/week | Evergreen |
| 🎥 Essay Clips | Strong opinion/idea moments | 30–90s | 2–3 per essay | Ideas |

*(Recommendation: don't over-brand the Essay Clips — let them feel like natural cuts from the long-form essays.)*

**Partnerships are a first-class lane**, not an afterthought. Media is the engine; coalition-building (universities, creator groups, AI labs, NGOs, policy forums) is how HDR becomes a *movement* rather than just a publication.

### Sister channels
Alongside HDR there are separately branded sister channels for the more speculative, philosophical side of the AI conversation — **CCA (Collective Conscious Agency)** and **IAG (Is AI God)**. These are run as a **separate workstream by Maria (Campaign Head)**; they're part of the bigger picture but operated independently, framed as a point of view rather than legal or technical fact. The main HDR voice stays neutral and research-backed. Cross-link, don't blend. **Abhishek and Sristi stay focused on HDR for now.**

## 9. Editorial Principles

**Every piece of media should be:** ✓ Human-first · ✓ Research-backed · ✓ Curious · ✓ Accessible · ✓ Neutral in tone · ✓ Solution-oriented.

**Avoid:** ✗ Fearmongering · ✗ Rage bait · ✗ Clickbait · ✗ Tribalism · ✗ AI hype · ✗ AI doomism.

**Sourcing:** every claim must be defendable and sourced before publishing. *(Note: a reference in the source material to a "Claude/Fable" model being banned in India could not be verified and must not be published without primary sourcing. Treat all sensational platform/company/legal claims this way.)*

## 10. Roadmap

- **Phase 1 — Editorial Foundation** *(now → Jul–Aug 2026):* stand up the weekly newsletter + video essay engine, launch the three recurring formats, build daily presence on X/Reddit and institutional presence on LinkedIn.
